SEEK's style guide details our visual and interactive language to ensure we have consistency and improved communication in our work across all product streams and platforms. By applying coherent design principles for all work, we ensure a high minimum standard is achieved, resulting in the protection of SEEK's brand and an improved experience for our users.

Using our styleguide

At SEEK, we like change, and it's anticipated that this guide will be regularly updated. It should include the most recent design templates and interactive patterns being developed by each team, ensuring that our work continues to evolve.

Design Framework

Colours

The SEEK product suite utilises a consistent, vibrant colour scheme and visual weight. Colours are designed to assist in clear visual communication and ensure accessibility on all devices and platforms.

Typography

Type should be beautifully functional, easily understood and scannable. Whether it be through size, colour, weight, kerning or letting, typography is absolutely critical to effective visual design and memorable user experiences.

Form Design

Forms are the key transactional layers that many people can loose sight of. For us, form design is a critical component that ensures we are always adhering to best practice design principles.

Design at SEEK

As a user, SEEK understands my needs and designs with empathy, applying decisions that are informed by beauty, logic, and data. Accessible design is usable across my devices, with consistent interactions, supported by clear affordance. A SEEK interaction is transparent and honest, requiring minimal thought to achieve my tasks. As designers we are true to SEEK's beliefs, focusing on business fundamentals and customer outcomes, not short term financials, determined to have a positive impact on society.

Design principles

SEEK have developed 10 principles that describe the fundamental goals the design team consider when applying their minds to new design challenges or refining existing work. Their purpose is to enable the creation of content that will assist our users to complete tasks easily and hopefully enjoy the experience.

Design history

SEEK has transformed its design practice in the past several years by doubling the number of User Experience team members in Product. This focus on design enables SEEK to deliver better and more consistent experiences for the our users of our products and services.
